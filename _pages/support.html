There are many resources available to help you with mental health issues. Here are a few:

Emergency Support

If you are in immediate danger, call 999 or go to your nearest Accident and Emergency department.

You can also call 111 or speak to your GP.

You can also:

  • Call Samaritans on 116 123 (UK-wide)
  • Text SHOUT to 85258 (UK-wide)
  • Call C.A.L.L. on 0800 132 737 (Wales)

More Information and Support

There are many organisations that can provide information and support for mental health issues. Here are a few:

Mind.org.uk

Mind provide access to a range of tools and services, including:

A-Z of Mental Health

National Health Service

The NHS provides a range of mental health services

CALM

The Campaign Against Living Miserably (CALM) provides information and hosts a webchat service and a private online forum for men bereaved by suicide.

Hub of Hope

Hub of Hope Lets you search for local, national, peer, community, charity, private and NHS mental health support. You can filter results to find specific kinds of support. They also have an app.

MindOut

You can also find support at sites like MindOut, who provide services run by and for LGBTQ+ people
